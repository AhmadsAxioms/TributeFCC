Sheikh Ibn Baz.

The Grand Mufti of Saudi Arabia

Abd al-Aziz ibn Abdullah ibn Baz

A short History and Achievements of the Mufti

  • Born: November 21, 1910, Riyadh, Emirate of Riyadh
  • Ibn Baz received a traditional education in Islamic literature with Islamic scholars.
  • In 1927, when he was sixteen, he started losing his eyesight after being afflicted with a serious infection in his eyes. By the time he was twenty, he had totally lost his sight and had become blind.
  • Judge of Al Kharj district upon the recommendation of Muhammad ibn 'Abdul-Lateef Al ash-Shaikh from 1938 to 1951.
  • In 1992 he was appointed Grand Mufti of Saudi Arabia and Head of the Council of Senior Scholars and was granted presidency of the administration for Permanent Committee for Scholarly Research and Ifta.
  • In 1981 he was awarded the King Faisal International Prize for Service to Islam.[13][14] He was the only Grand Mufti of Saudi Arabia not to come from the Al ash-Sheikh family.
  • Died: May 13, 1999, Mecca, Saudi Arabia
